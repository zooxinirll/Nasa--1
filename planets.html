The Solar System

The Sun
The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It's a massive ball of hydrogen and helium, undergoing nuclear fusion. This process releases the energy that sustains life on Earth.
Key Facts:
- Diameter: 1.39 million km
- Age: 4.6 billion years
- Surface Temperature: 5,500°C
- Core Temperature: 15 million °C
Mercury
Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar System and closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere to retain heat, leading to extreme temperature variations.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 88 Earth days
- Diameter: 4,880 km
- Surface Temperature: -173°C to 427°C
- No moons
Venus
Venus is similar in size to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere. It is the hottest planet in our Solar System due to the greenhouse effect caused by its carbon dioxide atmosphere.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 225 Earth days
- Diameter: 12,104 km
- Surface Temperature: 465°C (hotter than Mercury)
- No moons
Earth
Earth is the only planet known to support life, with its diverse climates, ecosystems, and oceans. It has one natural satellite: the Moon.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 365.25 days
- Diameter: 12,742 km
- Surface: 71% water
- One Moon
Mars
Mars, known as the "Red Planet" due to its iron oxide-rich surface, is home to the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 687 Earth days
- Diameter: 6,779 km
- Surface: Red iron oxide dust
- Two Moons: Phobos and Deimos
Jupiter
Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System, famous for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm. It has over 79 moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 12 Earth years
- Diameter: 139,820 km
- Great Red Spot: 1.3 times Earth's diameter
- 79 moons
Saturn
Saturn is known for its beautiful ring system, made up of ice, rock, and dust. It also has over 80 moons, the largest being Titan.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 29.5 Earth years
- Diameter: 116,460 km
- Rings: Composed of ice and rock
- 83 moons
Uranus
Uranus is unique due to its axial tilt, rotating on its side. It has faint rings and an atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen and helium.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 84 Earth years
- Diameter: 50,724 km
- Tilted axis of rotation
- 27 moons
Neptune
Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is known for its vivid blue color, caused by the presence of methane in its atmosphere. It's also the windiest planet, with storms stronger than on any other planet.
Key Facts:
- Orbital Period: 165 Earth years
- Diameter: 49,244 km
- Fastest winds: Over 2,000 km/h
- 14 moons
Asteroids
Asteroids are rocky objects that orbit the Sun, mostly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. They range in size from tiny pebbles to hundreds of kilometers in diameter.
Key Facts:
- Total Number: Millions
- Largest Asteroid: Ceres (diameter: 940 km)
- Composition: Mostly rock and metal
- Locations: Asteroid belt, near-Earth space
Moons and Satellites
A moon is a natural satellite that orbits a planet. Our Solar System has over 200 known moons, with each planet (except Mercury and Venus) having at least one moon. Jupiter’s moon Ganymede is the largest moon in the Solar System.
Key Facts:
- Total Moons: Over 200 known
- Largest Moon: Ganymede (Jupiter, 5,268 km diameter)
- Earth's Moon: Diameter of 3,474 km
- Many moons have atmospheres and active geology
